Martin Strauss

Research Scientist at Mirelo AI, developing multimodal foundation models for music and sound generation.

I’m a Research Scientist at Mirelo AI, working on multimodal foundation models for video, music, and sound. My core responsibilities include building our audio codecs, large-scale data pipelines, and model benchmarking and training, covering the full ML workflow from dataset preparation to distributed training.

I earned my Ph.D. (Dr.-Ing.) at the International Audio Laboratories Erlangen, a joint institution of Fraunhofer IIS and FAU Erlangen-Nürnberg, under Bernd Edler. I also spent time at Microsoft’s Applied Sciences Group, working on area-based sound source separation with Okan Köpüklü, contributed to dialogue-enhancement research at Fraunhofer IIS, and worked at INRIA in Rennes on sound source localisation using drones together with Antoine Deleforge.

Before specialising in audio AI, I earned a Master’s in Medical Image and Data Processing, a Bachelor’s in Medical Engineering, and earlier a Bachelor in Sports Science at TUM.
